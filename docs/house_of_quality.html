① Customer Requirements
Identify and list what customers value. Assign importance weights — they should sum to 100%. These drive every downstream calculation.
Σ Importance Weights = 100
② Relationship Matrix
How strongly does each engineering attribute satisfy each customer requirement? Click cells to cycle through values. Leave blank if no relationship exists.
9 = Strong   3 = Moderate   1 = Weak   · = None
The 9-3-1 geometric scale amplifies strong links and reduces noise from weak ones.
③ Priority Score
For each engineering attribute column j, multiply every relationship score by its row's importance weight, then sum. Higher scores = higher engineering priority.
Priority(j) = Σ [ Importance(i) × Relationship(i,j) ]
④ Relative Weight
Normalize priority scores to percentages. This tells engineers exactly where to allocate effort and investment across all attributes.
Weight(j) = Priority(j) ÷ Σ Priority × 100%
⑤ Competitive Evaluation
Rate each competitor and the new design against customer requirements on a 1–7 scale. Reveals gaps and differentiating opportunities.
1 = Not addressed    7 = Fully satisfied
⑥ Correlation Matrix (Roof)
Shows how engineering attributes interact. Critical for spotting trade-offs: improving one spec may help or harm another. Negative correlations demand design innovation.
+ = Positive synergy    − = Conflict / trade-off
